Samad Barri Khojasteh

I am a researcher at the University of Alcalá (UAH) in Madrid, Spain, where I work under the supervision of Prof. Daniel Pizarro and Prof. Adrien Bartoli. In 2025, I visited the EnCoV Research Group at the University of Clermont Auvergne, France, where I worked on non-rigid registration. Previously, I was a research intern at the University of Oviedo, Spain, under the Erasmus+ programme, supervised by Prof. José Ramón Villar.

Research

My research interests lie in applying machine learning and deep learning techniques to real-world healthcare challenges. I am currently working on Non-Rigid Structure from Motion (NRSfM), 3D reconstruction, and deformable modeling for medical imaging, with a particular focus on Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) and non-rigid registration. My research aims to develop high-fidelity and robust methods for reconstruction and registration in minimally invasive surgery (MIS). Previously, I worked on Human Activity Recognition (HAR), with an emphasis on fall detection for elderly care and monitoring.
